## About the Editor

Galelah Alawami is a graduate student in the Master of Library Science (MLIS) program at the [Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering](https://luddy.indiana.edu/) at [Indiana University Bloomington](https://www.indiana.edu/). She holds a Ph.D. in Curriculum and Instruction (Literacy) from the [University of Toledo](https://www.utoledo.edu/), a Master’s degree in Curriculum and Instruction from [Bowling Green State University](https://www.bgsu.edu/), and a Bachelor’s degree in Arabic Language from [King Saud University](https://ksu.edu.sa/).

Her doctoral dissertation, *[An Examination of Second Graders’ Engagement in Interactive Read-Alouds of Informational Texts Across Four Disciplines](https://books.google.com/books/about/An_Examination_of_Second_Graders_Engagem.html?id=LBzozwEACAAJ)* (University of Toledo, 2020), 

Alawami’s research interests include literacy education—especially reading children’s books—and the use of artificial intelligence (AI) in teaching and learning. She is also interested in archives and digital libraries, exploring how technology can support literacy development, educational research, and access to information in digital environments.
